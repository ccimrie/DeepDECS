DeepDECS

Framework for safer controllers

Autonomous systems have been utilising deep neural networks (DNNs) to perceive the world around them. However, no DNN is ever perfect, which can be devastating in safety-critical applications. DeepDECS is a developed solution able to capture the uncertainty of DNN perception components through the use of robustness verification methods From this DeepDECS produces safe autonomous system controllers for such applications.

The diagram on the right is the DeepDECS framework, and as can be seen DeepDECS requires four components from the user. First a discrete time Markov Chain (DTMC) of the application the autonomous system is to operate within, and second a requirements file defined via probabilistic computation tree logic (PCTL) notation. It is important to note that the DTMC provided will assume that the perception of the system is perfect. Lastly a pre trained perception DNN to be utilised in the autonomous system, and along an accompanying representative test dataset. These last two components are utilised, along with $n$ verification methods, in step 1 of DeepDECS to derive a set of $2^{n}$ confusion matrices. The accuracy derived from the confusion matrices will be the probabilities for our generated augmented DTMC, step 2 of DeepDECS. Our augmented model now contains an accurate representation of the imperfect perception unit. Step 3 of DeepDECS is controller synthesis, achieved via formal methods with the augmented DTMC and PCTL requirements.

DeepDECS Tutorial

How fast to drive on potential ice?

Say you have a autonomous car driving down a road and the car is equipped with a perception unit (a DNN). The aim of the perception unit is to determine if the next segment of road is icy or not. Having this information is invaluable as the car should not drive fast on icy roads, but the perception unit is not perfect so how do we best ascertain a safety critical controller?
